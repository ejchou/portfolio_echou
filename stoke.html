Stoke

Mindful Health Tracking

Problem

Stoke was created out of a need to make a comprehensive product that addresses the correlation amongst fitness, nutrition, and mental health. Analyses of competitors proved existing products on the market as one-sided, too heavily focused on hitting a number goal, and including data that is either misleading or too technical. Through user research, I learned users are left confused, overwhelmed, or traumatically triggered, making it difficult to lead a balanced and healthy lifestyle.

Mindfulness is at the core of Wellness.

    “Fitness and nutrition should add to your life, not be a negative thing.”

  1. The current state of the fitness and nutrition industry is rather triggering, especially for those with histories of disordered eating or body dysmorphia.

    2. “It would be great for the numbers to happen behind the scenes.”

  2. Users want their data to be more contextual and educational rather than generically prescribed numbers and graphs.

    3. “I struggled with that lean body mindset.”

  3. Positive encouragement is lacking in current trackers, which influences users to behave or pick up habits that do not improve health and wellbeing.

    4. “I think about eating quite a bit, and probably not in a healthy amount.”

  4. A large obstacle surrounding nutrition is proper emotional support and understanding how to affect stable positive change.

Solution

Stoke aims to provide realistic data and properly educate the user on how all their choices impact their health. The app seamlessly allows users to track all their health metrics and provides qualitative feedback.

Design Process

In order to start designing, I needed to determine the types of users that would benefit from Stoke. I created two user personas that reflected individuals with differing goals and education. While there is the average user who knows little of the science behind health, it was important to also consider users whose backgrounds are deeply rooted in fitness and nutrition.

Design Ideation

Now began the work to making a tangible product. To map out the layout of Stoke, I created user flows to come up with a sitemap. Card sorting was implemented to determine what needed clarification or revision. From there I focused on prototyping the dashboard and core features.

Constructing a design system and style guide allowed me to establish the app’s foundations and work out the components and patterns. To convey an emotional user experience, I utilized orange (excitement and enthusiasm) and green (tranquility and health) as my primary and secondary colors.

Iterations & Feedback

After several design iterations, usability testing was conducted with 5 participants to determine how easily users could understand the purpose and value of Stoke. A major insight from this testing was that users did not feel mindfulness was at the forefront of the app. As a core value, it was imperative to fix this issue. Additionally, I received peer reviews from fellow UX design students that aided in revising my design to become more useful and satisfactory.

Stoke v1
  • A dialog showing the user’s current wellness is the first thing users will see when they open the app.
  • Replaced title fills with vivid imagery to play to users’ emotions with appealing visuals.
  • Revised coloring to adhere to adequate contrasting.
  • Rounded all components to reduce austere UI.
  • Revised navigation menu to include labels.
  • The Stats feature is now a main screen and can be accessed at multiple points in the app.

Lessons & Next Steps

As Stoke evolved, my design became more comprehensible and personable. While it is still far from perfect, I’ve learned the importance of clarity for ease of use and using visuals to create a more satisfactory experience. Going forward, I will continue to prototype and test the remaining features. Keeping the core values and user personas in mind will aid me in staying on track to creating an effective solution within the health and wellness industry.
